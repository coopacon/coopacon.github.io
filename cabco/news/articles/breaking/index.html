Duke Energy Employee Becomes First Insurance Broker in New Company Division

Charlotte, NC — In a major step toward business diversification, Duke Energy has launched a new insurance arm, Duke Energy Insurance, and announced that longtime employee Gideon Cooper has become the company’s first licensed insurance broker.

Cooper, who served over 2 years in various engineering and service roles, now transitions into a customer-facing insurance role to lead policy sales and consultations for Duke’s North Carolina clientele.

“Duke is having me do insurance work for them now,” said Gideon Cooper. “What else do you want me to say?”

According to Duke Energy’s Vice President of Strategic Initiatives, Susan Parham, the move aims to address growing customer needs in climate-related coverage and renewable asset protection.

“This isn’t just a side business,” Parham noted. “Cooper brings deep operational insight that gives us a unique edge. I told him last Tuesday that he would be starting as an early-career frontrunning broker for Duke, and he told me 'OK', and then gave me a thumbs up. That's when I knew he had the exact attitude I was looking for.”

Duke Energy Insurance will offer a range of policies — including storm damage, solar panel coverage, and outage-loss protection, and of course: automobiles — tailored for residential and small business customers.

Cooper completed his state insurance certification earlier this year and will begin offering consultations as part of a soft launch in Mecklenburg, Wake, and Durham counties.

Policy sales are expected to open to the public this fall. Interested customers can sign up for updates or request early access at the official page below.
