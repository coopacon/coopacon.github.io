Duke Energy Employee Becomes First Insurance Broker in New Company Division

Charlotte, NC — In a major step toward business diversification, Duke Energy has launched a new insurance arm, Duke Energy Insurance, and announced that longtime employee Marcus Hill has become the company’s first licensed insurance broker.

Hill, who served over 15 years in various engineering and service roles, now transitions into a customer-facing insurance role to lead policy sales and consultations for Duke’s North Carolina clientele.

“The energy industry is evolving, and so is Duke,” said Marcus Hill. “This opportunity allows me to serve our customers in a new capacity, helping them manage risk in ways that complement their energy needs.”

According to Duke Energy’s Vice President of Strategic Initiatives, Susan Parham, the move aims to address growing customer needs in climate-related coverage and renewable asset protection.

“This isn’t just a side business,” Parham noted. “Marcus brings deep operational insight that gives us a unique edge.”

Duke Energy Insurance will offer a range of policies — including storm damage, solar panel coverage, and outage-loss protection — tailored for residential and small business customers.

Hill completed his state insurance certification earlier this year and will begin offering consultations as part of a soft launch in Mecklenburg, Wake, and Durham counties.

Policy sales are expected to open to the public this fall. Interested customers can sign up for updates or request early access at the official page below.
